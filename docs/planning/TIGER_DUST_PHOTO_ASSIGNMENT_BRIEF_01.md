# Tiger Dust v2 Photo Assignment Brief 01
Last reviewed: 2026-03-22 — content is accurate. No photography from this brief has been shot yet. This assignment is still pending (TD-049).

## Purpose

This brief covers the first photography pass needed to strengthen the Tiger Dust website and public brand presentation.

The objective is not to create an exhaustive archive yet.
The objective is to capture the minimum set of strong, intentional images needed to make the website feel real, current, useful, and visually confident.

---

# 1. Deliverable Goal

Produce a small, high-quality website image set that covers:
- storefront identity
- interior atmosphere
- human presence
- category-defining details
- practical visit/location utility

Target outcome:
A usable image library for the homepage, about page, visit page, and gallery/category sections.

---

# 2. Priority Shot List

## Priority A — Must Have

### A1. Storefront Hero
Capture a clean exterior image that clearly shows:
- storefront/window branding
- visual identity from the street
- enough context that a visitor recognizes the place

Variants to capture:
- straight-on exterior
- slight angle exterior
- wider environmental shot with storefront in context
- tighter signage/window crop

### A2. Signature Interior Wide
Capture at least one definitive interior-wide image that communicates:
- density
- wonder-room feeling
- layered inventory
- depth of space
- strong atmosphere without looking chaotic in a bad way

Variants:
- wide from front toward back
- wide from deeper in the shop toward entrance
- one version emphasizing ceiling/hanging decor if strong

### A3. “Real Place, Real People” Image
Capture at least one image with human presence.

Ideal options:
- Laura in the shop or studio
- Jason/Laura interacting in the space
- staff/customer browsing moment
- counter interaction

Goal:
Keep the website from feeling like a disembodied object catalog.

### A4. Visit/Maps Utility Shot
Capture one image that works specifically for “Visit Us.”

Requirements:
- clear entrance orientation
- readable storefront cues
- useful for someone trying to recognize the location

# 3. Art Direction

## Tone
The images should feel:
- curated
- atmospheric
- rich
- strange
- warm
- tactile
- lived-in

Avoid making the shop feel:
- sterile
- over-lit retail
- generic ecommerce
- cluttered without intent

## Visual balance
Aim for a mix of:
- wide environmental shots
- medium vignettes
- tight object/detail shots
- at least one human-centered image

## Brand read
Every final image set should reinforce some combination of:
- wunderkammer
- always Halloween
- treasure hunt
- vintage + occult + playful
- real brick-and-mortar destination

---

# 4. Framing Guidance

## For wides
- watch edge clutter
- avoid dead empty floor dominating frame
- preserve density without losing legibility
- keep verticals honest unless stylization is intentional

## For detail shots
- choose category-defining subjects
- prefer strong textures and shapes
- avoid visual noise that doesn’t communicate anything

## For people shots
- keep them natural and grounded
- no fake corporate-smile energy
- better to feel candid and real than staged and hollow

---

# 5. Practical Capture Notes

## Lighting
If possible, capture:
- one daylight storefront set
- one warmer interior set when the space feels most alive

## Orientation mix
Capture both:
- landscape for hero/section use
- portrait/vertical where useful for social/mobile crops

## Coverage mindset
For each priority setup, try to get:
- safe shot
- best shot
- alternate crop/composition

---

# 6. Minimum Viable Delivery Set

A good first-pass delivery could be as small as:
- 2 storefront images
- 2 interior wides
- 1 human-presence image
- 4 category/detail images
- 1 visit-oriented exterior image
- 2 texture/support images

Total minimum useful set:
**12 strong images**

That is enough to materially improve the site.

# 8. Definition of Success

This shoot is successful if, after delivery:
- the website feels clearly more alive
- the shop is easier to recognize and visit
- the brand reads as curated and specific
- the human authorship of the space becomes more visible
- at least six category/image roles are covered without needing filler
